heading: "Management of Unusual Sources of Upper GI Bleeding"
---

Recognizing unusual sources of upper GI bleeding is important in evaluating individual patients who present diagnostic dilemmas and in promptly managing patients with uncommon but potentially catastrophic bleeding sources. As upper GI bleeds are relatively common, the probability of encountering a patient with an unusual source of bleeding is likely at some point; therefore, understanding the diagnosis and management of these uncommon pathologic processes is essential in caring for patients with upper gastrointestinal bleeding. In this chapter, we will discuss the diagnosis and management of gastric antral vascular ectasia (GA VE), aortoduodenal fistula (ADF), arteriovenous malformation (AVM), and hemorrhagic gastritis.
