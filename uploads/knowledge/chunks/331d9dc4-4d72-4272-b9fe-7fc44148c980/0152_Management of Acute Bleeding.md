It produces selective splanchnic vasoconstriction and decreases azygous blood flow, a surrogate marker of variceal blood flow. Octreotide treatment is instituted as a bolus dose followed by a 3-5 day continuous infusion. Terlipressin is an analogue of the natural hormone arginine vasopressin. It stimulates vasopressin-1 receptors, which are located in vascular smooth muscle and mediate vasoconstriction. Administration of terlipressin increases mean arterial pressure and decreases portal flow and pressure, which ultimately result in improvement of management of bleeding esophageal varices [11]. A Cochrane review of studies investigating Terlipressin demonstrated 34% relative risk reduction in mortality [12].
