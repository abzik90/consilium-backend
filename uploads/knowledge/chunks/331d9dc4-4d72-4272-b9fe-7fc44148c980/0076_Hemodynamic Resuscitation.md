heading: "Hemodynamic Resuscitation"
---

It has been demonstrated that, of the modifiable factors affecting outcome, prompt hemodynamic resuscitation affords decreased mortality, as well as morbidity in the form of reduced incidence of myocardial infarction [25]. These authors thus recommend prompt resuscitation of adequate hemodynamic parameters and correction of hematocrit and coagulopathy. We also support this recommendation and thus the initial evaluation begins with a prompt assessment of hemodynamic instability and aggressive resuscitation afforded within a critical care setting. Delays in resuscitation lead to delays in therapeutic interventions, and thus increased morbidity [25, 26].
