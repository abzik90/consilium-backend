heading: "Guaiac-Based Tests"
---

Guaiac-based fecal occult blood testing is the most commonly used testing method because of its simplicity. Guaiac is a colorless compound that turns blue on exposure to hemoglobin. Guaiac tests are more sensitive for the detection of bleeding from the lower gastrointestinal tract than from the upper gastrointestinal tract because hemoglobin is degraded as it travels down the gastrointestinal tract [7]. The likelihood that a guaiac test will detect fecal occult blood is related to the quantity of hemoglobin in the stool, which in turn is affected by the size and location of the bleeding lesion [8].
