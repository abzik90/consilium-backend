The technique is illustrated in Fig. 5 and first involves maximal advancement of the enteroscope through the overtube into the small bowel; the enteroscope position is then fixed by inflating its balloon. The overtube may then be advanced distally over the scope and is then also fixed by inflating its balloon. As both balloons are inflated, the enteroscope and overtube are retracted as a unit to pleat the bowel over the instrument. Then the enteroscope balloon is deflated and the scope is further inserted. Once it is fully advanced, the enteroscope balloon is inflated once again and the overtube balloon may be deflated, allowing advancement of the overtube once again over the scope.
