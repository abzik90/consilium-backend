Laboratory values, such as complete blood count (CBC), coagulation markers, liver function tests, and a basic metabolic panel, including BUN and creatinine, should be obtained. A thorough abdominal exam should be performed to ascertain tenderness and to rule out peritoneal signs, which would be cause for immediate operative management. A complete rectal exam is performed to look for rectal causes of GI bleeding.
