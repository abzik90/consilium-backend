Both capsule endoscopy and double-balloon enteroscopy are exciting new tools in the diagnosis and treatment of obscure gastrointestinal bleeding as well as many other diseases of the small bowel. Comparisons of these two modalities have shown them to have similar diagnostic rates, although neither is perfect. For example, in a study by Hadithi et al., 115 patients underwent both VCE and DBE and the calculated miss rates were 20 and 28%, respectively [41].
