Heparin infusion has been used to unmask or amplify bleeding in order to identify a bleeding site angiographically. Following a papaverine augmented angiogram identifying 6 out of 18 GI bleeding patients, 24 h heparin administration in the remaining patients localized six additional bleeds. Thus, 12 out of 18 bleeds (67%) were localized angiographically by Mernagh [4]. This is similar to the yield obtained by Koval.
