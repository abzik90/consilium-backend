Finally, a meta-analysis comparing stapled hemorrhoidopexy with conventional operative techniques including 29 randomized trials of 2,056 patients demonstrated that the stapled technique was less painful, required a shorter inpatient hospital stay, could be performed more quickly, and allowed patients to return to normal activities sooner. Complication rates were not significantly different. Unfortunately, the stapled technique resulted in a higher rate of recurrent disease (relative risk 2.29). It was also noted that the learning curve to perform the stapled procedure independently is at least 10 cases [27].
