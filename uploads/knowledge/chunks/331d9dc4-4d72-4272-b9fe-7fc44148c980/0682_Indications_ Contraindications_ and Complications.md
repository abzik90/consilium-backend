In an effort to reduce this risk, a patency capsule has been developed by Given Imaging (Agile Patency System) that contains lactose with 10% barium and a radiofrequency identification tag that may be detected by a matching scanner and is taken in the same way as the normal capsule; its location is assessed within 30 h by either fluoroscopy or the scanner. Abdominal pain may occur in up to 14% of patients with known strictures with the use of these patency capsules; however, no obstruction has been reported as the capsule will dissolve in less than 80 h [12, 13]. Plain abdominal x-rays are usually recommended after 2 weeks to assess for retention if the patient has been unaware of passing the capsule in their stool; removal is either surgical although there are reports of double-balloon enteroscopy being used to retrieve capsules.
