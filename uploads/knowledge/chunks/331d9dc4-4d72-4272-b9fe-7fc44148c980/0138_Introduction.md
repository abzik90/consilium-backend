Gastroesophageal varices are the classic collateral pathways that develop from the left gastric and short gastric veins to the lesser and greater curves of the stomach. These veins then run submucosally across the gastroesophageal junction up the esophagus (Fig. 1). Additional collateral pathways are found in the periumbilical, retroperitoneal, and hemorrhoidal venous plexuses.
