Both wireless capsule endoscopy and double-balloon enteroscopy are relatively recent technologies, both becoming commercially available in the West in 2003, that have been developed to answer the need for more effective diagnostic and therapeutic access to the small bowel. This chapter will summarize the data currently available on the two technologies and outline their place in the algorithm of managing obscure gastrointestinal bleeding.
