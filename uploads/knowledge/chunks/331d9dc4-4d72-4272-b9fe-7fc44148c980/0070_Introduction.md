Dieulafoy lesions are congenitally enlarged submucosal arteries that account for approximately 2% of non-variceal UGIB [12]. The vast majority are less than 5 mm and located 6-10 cm below the gastroesophageal junction on the lesser curvature [13]. Several endoscopic therapies are utilized for bleeding control, but up to 20% of patients may require surgery for recurrent bleeding [14] (Fig. 3).
