Although once managed surgically, there is now consistent recommendation within the literature that endoscopy is the treatment of choice for Dieulafoy's lesions [12, 11, 17-19]. Reports in the last 8 years (Table 1) have described successful treatment with epinephrine injection, heat probe coagulation, mechanical methods (banding, hemoclipping), and various combinations. Hemostasis with other methods, including laser photocoagulation and sclerosing agents, has also been described [6]. Small retrospective and randomized controlled trials have demonstrated a decreased risk of recurrent bleeding when 'mechanical' hemostasis (hemoclipping or band ligation) is used, compared with epinephrine injection [21, 23, 24]. Park et al. found no difference between patients treated with hemoclipping vs. with band ligation [16]: rates of hemostasis and recurrence were equal among the two groups.
