The annual rate of hospitalization for acute upper gastrointestinal hemorrhage in the United States is estimated to be 160 hospital admissions per 100,000 population, which translates into more than 400,000 per year [12]. In most settings, the vast majority of acute episodes of upper gastrointestinal bleeding (80-90%) have nonvariceal causes, with gastroduodenal peptic ulcers accounting for the majority of lesions [13].
