heading: "Fecal Occult Blood"
---

Gastrointestinal blood loss must exceed 10 ml/day (normal <2 ml/day) to produce a positive fecal occult blood test. This degree of bleeding (10 ml/day) will produce a positive test only 50% of the time by most testing methods [6]. Multiple factors determine the likelihood of detecting fecal occult blood. These factors include the sensitivity of the particular test which is being used, the anatomic level of bleeding, the frequency and rate at which the causative lesion bleeds, and bowel motility. All of these factors influence the intraluminal metabolism of hemoglobin.
