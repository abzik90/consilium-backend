Based upon the available data, an assumption that reteplase may be the optimal agent for the induction of bleeding may be considered. However, equivalent dosing of the medications as well as other factors may have contributed to the variability in sensitivities. Of the fibrinolytic agents available, reteplase has lower fibrin specificity than tPA, however, it has superior clot penetration. Likely, the economic impact of stocking these medications in pharmacy formularies precludes the availability of both at a single institution. Notably, tolazoline is no longer available in our market, and nitroglycerin has replaced it.
