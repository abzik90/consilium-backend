heading: "Management of Acute Bleeding"
---

Surgical shunts can be broadly divided into three categories: non-selective, selective, and partial shunts. Non-selective shunts completely eliminate hepatic portal perfusion. Selective shunts decompress esophageal and gastric varices but maintain portal flow to the liver. Partial shunts preserve some portal flow and incompletely decompress the portal venous system.
