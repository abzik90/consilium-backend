This nuclear medicine study is often chosen because it is non-invasive and without the risks of angiography or colonoscopy. However, it is rarely sufficient for definitive diagnosis and often leads to either colonoscopy or angiography. There is no therapeutic component to scintigraphy, as it is a strictly diagnostic modality. Because of its low resolution, it can be difficult to pinpoint the location of the bleeding, and it is generally not recommended that segmental resection be performed solely on the basis of scintigraphy results. Its use, therefore, is limited in the urgent setting and should be implemented based on clinical judgment for an individual patient who may not be a suitable candidate for either colonoscopy or angiography.
