heading: "Management of Acute Bleeding"
---

The two basic non-selective shunts are the end-to-side portacaval shunt (Eck fistula) which directly diverts all portal flow to the inferior vena cava and the sideto-side shunt which preserves portal perfusion while decompressing varices. While highly effective in reducing the incidence of bleeding and ascites, they are both associated with a very high rate of liver dysfunction due to reduced hepatic blood flow. With the advent of TIPS, there are few indications for non-selective shunts.
