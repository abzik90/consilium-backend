The use of high-dose oral proton-pump inhibitors in peptic ulcer bleeding has been shown in Asian populations to lead to reductions in the risk of rebleeding (OR 0.24, 95% CI 0.16-0.26), the need for surgery (OR 0.29, 95% CI 0.16-0.53), and the risk of death (OR 0.35, 95% CI 0.16-0.74) [84]. However, these results may not be generalizable to North American or European populations because of underlying differences in physiological measures and pharmacodynamic profiles, and prevalence rates of H. pylori infection, factors that may favor the acid-suppressive effect of a given dose of a proton-pump inhibitor in Asian patients [84].
