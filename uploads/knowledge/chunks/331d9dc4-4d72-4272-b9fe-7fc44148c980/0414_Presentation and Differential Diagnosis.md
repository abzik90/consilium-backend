heading: "Presentation and Differential Diagnosis"
---

Bleeding from the biliary tree may present with the classic triad of upper GI bleeding (hematemesis or melena), jaundice, and right upper quadrant abdominal pain. However, this triad is only seen in approximately one-third of patients [2, 4]. While the majority of patients who develop hemobilia remain asymptomatic, hemobilia can be lethal [5, 10]. Depending on the etiology and nature of the bleeding, hemobilia may present acutely or it may have an insidious and seemingly obscure presentation. When biliary tract bleeding evolves after a diagnostic or therapeutic intervention, the presentation may be subtle but the diagnosis is generally obvious
