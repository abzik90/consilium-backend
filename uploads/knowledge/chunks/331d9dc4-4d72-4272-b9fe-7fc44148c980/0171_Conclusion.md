Bleeding from esophageal varices as a sequelae of liver disease and portal hypertension is a potentially life-threatening problem. Treatment modalities are many and, in part, depend on the stability and underlying disease process of the patient. Once stabilized in the acute setting, the goal of treatment is to control current bleeding, prevent associated complications, and reduce the risk of future bleeding. Due to the high-risk patient population and the advent of newer technologies, endoscopic and interventional radiology treatments of variceal bleeding have become the mainstays of therapy. While surgical treatments still exist, they are used less frequently due to failure of improvement in long-term survival.
