Histologically, a Dieulafoy's lesion is not aneurismal, but instead an enlarged, tortuous artery lying just under the gastrointestinal mucosa, with only a minute defect at the point of contact with the mucosa [4, 5] (Fig. 1). The vessel is typically 1-3 mm in diameter, significantly larger than vessels in neighboring submucosa.
