In most institutions, radiologic intervention is reserved for patients in whom endoscopic therapy has failed, especially if such patients are high-risk surgical candidates. A retrospective analysis showed no significant difference between embolization therapy and surgery in the incidence of recurrent bleeding (29 and 23.1%, respectively), the need for additional surgery (16.1 and 30.8%), and mortality (25.8 and 20.5%), despite a more advanced age and higher prevalence of heart disease in the group receiving embolization therapy [109].
