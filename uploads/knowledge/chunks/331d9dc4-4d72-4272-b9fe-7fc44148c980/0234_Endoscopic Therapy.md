However, the resources necessary to perform repeated endoscopies are not inconsequential, and the precise role for fibrin sealant in this setting remains to be defined. While injection therapy is a cornerstone of endoscopic treatment of bleeding peptic ulcers, the rebleeding rate is high (approximately 18%) if injection therapy alone is performed [50]. Injection therapy alone was shown to be less effective at preventing recurrent bleeding compared to bipolar electrocoagulation in a randomized controlled trial involving 100 patients with high-risk bleeding ulcers [51].
