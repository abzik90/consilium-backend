Multiple small trials have reported excellent results with the application of hemoclips (Figs. 4a, 4b) to Mallory-Weiss tears [16-18]. These studies reported excellent hemostasis and minimal to no re-bleeding. Despite this, a recent meta-analysis of endoscopic clipping for acute non-variceal upper gastrointestinal bleeding reported that clipping alone was not superior to other endoscopic modalities [19]. These results may be slightly skewed due to the fact this analysis contained a majority of patients with lesions other than Mallory-Weiss tears. Variceal banding has been reported for treatment of Mallory-Weiss tears with good results for these patients [18]. Just as with treatment of bleeding ulcers, multiple endoscopic therapeutic techniques have been shown to be effective and the technique used should probably be dependent upon the experience and comfort level of the endoscopist.
