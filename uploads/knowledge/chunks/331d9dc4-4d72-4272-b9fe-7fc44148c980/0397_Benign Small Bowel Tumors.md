Nodular lymphoid hyperplasia. It is an obscure benign cause of GI bleeding which has been described primarily in the pediatric and young adult population. Multiple large lymphoid follicles give the appearance of submucosal masses, and these enlarged lymphoid follicles may result in malabsorption and diarrhea. The condition generally affects the distal ileum and jejunum [6]. Patients may present with melena or hematochezia, and rarely massive GI bleeding. Treatment involves resection of the involved segments of bowel [50].
