Once a bleeding source is identified, several treatment modalities have been found to be effective. For bleeding diverticula, heat probe, bipolar or multipolar coagulation, or epinephrine injections can be used independently or concurrently. Endoscopic metallic clip placement is an alternative, but requires visualization of a vessel. It is actually uncommon to easily identify a bleeding vessel or stigmata of recent bleeding, such as adherent clot. More often, there is a presumed area of concern that can be treated (Fig. 2). For A VMs, both thermal and epinephrine injections are quite successful. In November 2005, the American Society for Gastrointestinal Endoscopy issued guidelines for LGIB recommending early colonoscopy and reviewed the different treatment modalities [11].
