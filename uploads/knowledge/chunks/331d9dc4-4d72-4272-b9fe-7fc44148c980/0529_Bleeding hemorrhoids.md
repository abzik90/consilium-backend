heading: "Bleeding hemorrhoids"

Wang et al. described a modified Ferguson technique for treating circumferential prolapsed grade III or IV hemorrhoids in 738 patients. They radically resected hemorrhoidal tissues and redundant anoderm before anchoring the residual anoderm and mucosa to the internal sphincter above the dentate line in a tension-free manner. Mean operative time was 27 min and hospital stay was 3.4 days. Their complication rates, including anal stenosis and incontinence, were comparable to other radical techniques. Their incidence of hemorrhoidal recurrence and postoperative pain was less than with other radical techniques making this a reasonable alternative to consider [24].
