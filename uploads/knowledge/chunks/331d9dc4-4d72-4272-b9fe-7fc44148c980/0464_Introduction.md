The etiology of LGIB varies widely by age, and the epidemiology of the disease must be considered in arriving at the correct diagnosis (Table 1). The most common cause of LGIB is colonic diverticulosis, which accounts for up to 60% of all LGIB. In children, however, Meckel's diverticulum, a congenital anomaly of the small bowel, and intussusception are common sources of LGIB [2]. In adults older than 60 years of age, angiodysplasia may be slightly more common than diverticular disease. Other frequent causes of lower GI bleeding are colitis from inflammatory bowel disease, infection or ischemia, neoplasms, polyps, anorectal disease such as hemorrhoids or anal fissures, and mesenteric ischemia.
