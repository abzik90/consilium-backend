With aging, the presence of angiodysplasia (or arteriovenous malformations AVM)increases. By the age of 65, AVM may be the most common source of colonic bleeding. AVM are vascular anomalies of the gastrointestinal tract characterized by dilated and tortuous submucosal vessels. AVM may be congenital, but more often these develop over time, possibly from chronic venous obstruction, chronic mucosal ischemia, or as a complication of other comorbidities such as cardiac, vascular, or pulmonary disease. There is an association between AVM and aortic stenosis; however, a causal role has not been revealed [4]. In contrast to diverticular disease, colonic AVM are most often located in the right colon.
