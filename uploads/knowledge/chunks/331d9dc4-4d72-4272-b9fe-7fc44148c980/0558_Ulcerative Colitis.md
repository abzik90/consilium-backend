Cyclosporine has been used as a final therapeutic measure prior to colectomy in patients with bleeding refractory to high-dose intravenous steroids; however, it may take up to a week for patients to have a significant improvement in symptomatology. Cyclosporine is also nephrotoxic and may not be a therapeutic measure that is of much benefit overall since most patients receiving cyclosporine eventually progress to requiring a colectomy within a year.
