Barium enema may play a role in the evaluation of a suspected colonic tumor, but really plays no role in the assessment of severe hematochezia. It fails to identify the hemorrhagic source in up to 30% of cases [3, 28]. Even when positive, there is no guarantee that the visualized lesion is also the bleeding culprit. Finally, the barium itself interferes significantly with future evaluations, such as colonoscopy or angiography.
