heading: "Pharmacological Adjuncts"
---

Octreotide, a somatostatin analogue, is traditionally used to reduce the risk of recurrent variceal bleeding, though recently it has been indicated in non-variceal bleeding, as it not only reduces splanchnic perfusion, but also reduces gastric acid secretion and may have a gastric cytoprotective effect and protects renal flow [43].
