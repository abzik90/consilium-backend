heading: "Colonoscopy"
---

Inadequate bowel preparation may lead to incomplete evaluation of the colonic mucosa. Colonoscopy may be also ineffective in the face of active bleeding and insufflation of air may obliterate the lesions. In addition, blood pressure and volume status can alter the colonoscopic appearance of vascular lesions as it is the use of narcotic medications for sedation and analgesia. Studies have shown that administration of intravenous naloxone enhances the appearance of AVM during colonoscopy in patients who have received meperidine for sedation [14].
