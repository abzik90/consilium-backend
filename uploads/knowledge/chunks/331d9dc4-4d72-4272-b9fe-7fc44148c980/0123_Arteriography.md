heading: "Arteriography"
---

In spite of the many benefits of arteriography in the detection of occult upper GI bleeding, there is the potential for complications. Arterial injury, contrast reactions, nephrotoxicity, thromboemboli, and hemorrhage are possible but occur quite infrequently. Arteriograms for upper or lower GI bleeding have a complication rate of <5% [17]. Relative contraindications to the procedure include severe coagulopathy, congestive heart failure, recent myocardial infarction, renal insufficiency, and pregnancy [19].
