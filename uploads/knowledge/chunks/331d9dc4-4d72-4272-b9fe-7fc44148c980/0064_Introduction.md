UGIB are estimated to result in 40-150 episodes per 100,000 population [3], resulting in more than 300,000 hospital admissions [4] and accounting for 1-2% of all annual US hospital admissions [5]. At least 50% of UGIB result from peptic ulcer disease [6] (Fig. 1), even amongst those with sequelae from advanced liver disease [7]. This diagnosis is followed more than 10% of the time by variceal bleeding, erosive disease, and Mallory-Weiss tears, and less commonly by diagnoses such as angiodysplasias, posttraumatic, neoplasms, and Dieulafoy lesions [8, 9].
