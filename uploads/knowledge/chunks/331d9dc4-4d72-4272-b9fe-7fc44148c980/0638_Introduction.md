- Occult bleeding: initial presentation of a positive fecal occult blood test (FOBT) results and/or iron-deficiency anemia, when there is no evidence of visible blood to the patient or physician.
- Obscure bleeding: bleeding from the gastrointestinal tract that persists or recurs without an obvious etiology after upper endoscopy, colonoscopy, and radiological evaluation of the small bowel (such as by small bowel follow through or enteroclysis).
