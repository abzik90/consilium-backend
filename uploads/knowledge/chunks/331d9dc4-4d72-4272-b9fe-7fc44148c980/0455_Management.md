Upon confirmation of HP, intervention generally consists of a combination of endovascular control of arterial hemorrhage and subsequent operative intervention. Hemodynamically stable patients can be temporized with endovascular techniques for control of hemorrhage. Interventional radiographic methods provide the optimal first line of treatment. Transcatheter balloon occlusion, coil embolization, and vascular stent deployment are several distinct methods for treatment. Balloon occlusion obstructs the artery prior to surgery, limiting blood loss and shortening operative times [1]. Coil embolization induces thrombus formation within the diseased vessel
