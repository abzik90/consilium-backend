Small bowel plain films. These are the most widely used studies for small intestine disease and generally include both flat and upright views of the abdomen. Evidence of dilated small bowel loops and the presence of air-fluid levels may suggest an area of obstruction secondary to a tumor. The presence of free air on upright views suggests perforation of a hollow viscous. In addition, a neoplasm may rarely be evident as calcified mass on plain film.
