heading: "Presentation and Differential Diagnosis"
---

Liver injury with late hemobilia that develops after blunt trauma can often be missed. Therefore, when hemobilia occurs after trauma, one must have a higher index of suspicion. Particularly, if one has decided to proceed with non-operative management after blunt hepatic trauma. Subtle clues may involve rising bilirubin or evolving anemia or both. The presentation of hemobilia following trauma may be as early as 4 days after the injury but can also occur as late as a month following the event [3]. Occasionally, the symptoms may involve pain and hematemesis after decompression of an intrahepatic hematoma into the biliary system.
