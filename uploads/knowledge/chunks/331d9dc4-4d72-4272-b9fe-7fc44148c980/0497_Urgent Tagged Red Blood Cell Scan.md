Technetium 99m-labeled erythrocyte scintigraphy may be able to detect hemorrhage at rates as low as 0.1 mL/min, thus can be more sensitive than angiography in slower bleeds. The published sensitivity is incredibly variable and ranges from 26 to 82%. False-positive studies may lead to inappropriate surgery, and false negatives lead to diagnostic delays. Of the positive scans (Fig. 6), location of bleeding is inaccurate 20-25% of the time, which can also lead to inappropriate surgeries [14].
