Establishing the diagnosis of colonic diverticular disease starts with a complete history and physical examination. The presence of any associated comorbidity, family history of bleeding disorders, cancer, medications, and date of last colonoscopic examination should be investigated. Physical examination can demonstrate tachycardia, hypotension, and postural changes based on the amount of blood lost.
