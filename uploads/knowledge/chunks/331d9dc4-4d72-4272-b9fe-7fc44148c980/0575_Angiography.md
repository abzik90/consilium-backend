heading: "Angiography"
---

Angiography remains an excellent means for the diagnosis and treatment of bleeding diverticular disease. The overall sensitivity of angiography ranges from 58 to 86% and detection of bleeding depends on the rate of bleeding (as low as 0.5 mL/min) and localization. Selective mesenteric angiography should be reserved for those patients in whom colonoscopy is not appropriate [1] or in patients who are actively bleeding.
