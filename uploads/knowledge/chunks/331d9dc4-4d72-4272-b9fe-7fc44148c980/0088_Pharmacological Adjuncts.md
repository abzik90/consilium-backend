Intravenous proton pump inhibitors (PPI) are recommended when a diagnosis of a UGIB is made and one should not wait until confirmation of the source to be a peptic ulcer. Not only does this strategy reduce total length of hospital stay, but it aids the endoscopist as there are fewer actively bleeding ulcers and more ulcers without stigmata of recent bleeding [44]. Opponents of this strategy cite the additional costs associated with this costly therapy in patients without bleeding sources related to acid secretion; however, recent cost analyses have demonstrated this strategy to be more effective pre-endoscopy than afterwards and a less costly treatment strategy for all UGIB [18, 45].
