Provided the patient is hemodynamically stable, it is useful to try and narrow the potential etiologies prior to engaging in diagnostic procedures, as the differential diagnosis for OGIB is extensive. Detailed medical history regarding prior episodes, travel history, concomitant medical conditions such as pancreatic disease, coagulopathies, HIV status, and prior surgical procedures including vascular and gastrointestinal bypass surgery are important. A detailed approach to the history can often define the appropriate diagnostic approach to the patient and improve the chances of an accurate diagnosis.
