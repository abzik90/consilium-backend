Arteriovenous malformations have been associated with aortic stenosis. Heyde first reported this association in 1958, describing the Heyde syndrome as the combination of aortic stenosis and GI bleeding due to colonic A VM. These lesions are also notably associated with renal failure, especially in elderly patients, and patients with von Willebrand disease have an increased incidence of gastrointestinal bleeding from colonic AVM.
