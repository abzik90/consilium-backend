Our provocative angiography protocol involves first performing conventional diagnostic mesenteric angiography on fore-, mid-, and hindgut distributions (celiac, superior mesenteric, and inferior mesenteric arteries). Unless the patient is unstable, the majority of patients have undergone scintigraphic imaging to determine the vascular distribution of concern. In the event scintigraphy has identified a bleeding site, the relevant vascular distribution is targeted for provocation. If there is no bleeding on scintigraphy, the superior mesenteric artery is targeted due to the opportunity to cover a greater length of the lower GI tract with vasodilator and fibrinolytic. Others such as Wildus [8] have suggested starting with the inferior mesenteric artery, due to the relative difficulty entering this vessel, as compared to the superior mesenteric and celiac distributions.
