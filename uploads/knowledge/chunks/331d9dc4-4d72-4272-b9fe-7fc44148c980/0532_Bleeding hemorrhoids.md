Recurrence rates are approximately 18% and recurrences tend to be of lesser severity. If repeat intervention is required for recurrent hemorrhoids, minimally invasive techniques are usually sufficient. Care must be paid to prevent serious complications using this technique. If performed on a woman, operative procedure must include concomitant vaginal exploration to prevent inclusion of vaginal tissue in the staple line [28]. Similarly, attention must be paid to prevent complete occlusion of the anal opening with the stapler. Devastating complications have been rarely reported including retroperitoneal sepsis [29, 30]. Care must also be paid to proper placement of the staple line sufficiently above the dentate line to prevent serious post-procedural pain syndromes inherent in stapling the sensitized anoderm. Finally, cost is also a consideration with stapled hemorrhoidopexy being more expensive than traditional techniques due to cost of the stapling device [31].
