heading: "Management of Acute Bleeding"
---

Patients present with bloody emesis, which can be brisk, often requiring ICU admission. Bleeding esophageal varices are somewhat unique, in that no single physician can perform every procedure possibly required by the patient, but must understand the role of and indications for each intervention. Thus, management requires a coordinated multidisciplinary effort, with clear communication between critical care physicians, gastroenterologists, surgeons, and vascular radiologists for optimal patient outcomes.
