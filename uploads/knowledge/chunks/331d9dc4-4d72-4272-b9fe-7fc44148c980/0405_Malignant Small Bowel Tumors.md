heading: "Malignant Small Bowel Tumors"
---

Carcinoid tumors. Carcinoid tumors account for approximately 25-30% of small bowel tumors; however, they are a rare cause of small bowel bleeding [6]. These tumors arise from the enterochromaffin cell of the gut and, in the small bowel, most commonly localize to the ileum (52%) [7]. If detected in the evaluation of a bleeding small bowel tumor, a careful evaluation of the entire small bowel is necessary to exclude the presence of multiple synchronous carcinoid nodules [59].
