Approximately 90% of the time, the source of GI hemorrhage is localized and in 70% of cases, bleeding can be successfully treated or temporized by endoscopic or angiographic intervention (Figs. 1, 2, 3, and 4) Improvements in these techniques have allowed upfront damage control surgery to become the exception rather than the rule for patients with acute GIB. For patient outcomes, this represents a huge impact in that 'blind' segmental resections based on suspicion alone are associated not only with high rates of recurrent hemorrhage but also with high rates of morbidity and mortality [2, 16, 29-31].
