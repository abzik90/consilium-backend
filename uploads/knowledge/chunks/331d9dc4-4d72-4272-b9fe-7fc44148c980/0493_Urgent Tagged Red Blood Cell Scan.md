The most controversial of the three modalities in the urgent setting is radionucleotide scintigraphy. Technetium 99m-labeled erythrocyte scintigraphy is the most widely available, used and preferred because of its long half-life and its usefulness in delayed images. Alternate molecules have been used for scintigraphy, such as sulfur colloid and albumin. Technetium 99m-sulfur colloid is unavailable in many areas; the sulfur colloid molecule is rapidly cleared from the vasculature into bone marrow, liver, and spleen.
