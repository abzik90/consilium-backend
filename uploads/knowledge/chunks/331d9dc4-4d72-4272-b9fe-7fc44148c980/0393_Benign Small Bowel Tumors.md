Lipoma. Lipomas are the second most common benign tumor of the small bowel with very little malignant potential. These are usually solitary tumors, ranging in size from 1 to 30 cm [43]. More that two-thirds of lipomas are asymptomatic; however, large tumors may be associated with bleeding, as well as small bowel obstruction or intussusception [3]. While lipomas less than 1 cm rarely cause symptoms, 75% of lipomas larger than 4 cm are symptomatic [44]. Most frequently, lipomas are located distally in the small bowel and in the terminal ileum, with the jejunum being the least common location.
