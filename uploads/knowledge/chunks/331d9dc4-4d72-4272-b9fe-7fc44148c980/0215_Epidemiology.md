Moreover, peptic ulcer bleeding is seen predominantly among the elderly, with 68% of patients over the age of 60 years and 27% over the age of 80 years [16]. Mortality associated with peptic ulcer bleeding remains high at 5-10% [13]. Estimated direct medical costs for the in-hospital care of patients with bleeding from a peptic ulcer total more than $2 billion annually in the United States [17].
