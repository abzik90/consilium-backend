heading: "Surgical Approach"
---

If previous endoscopic and angiographic attempts have failed, surgery provides the definitive treatment for ongoing or recurrent diverticular disease bleeding. Indications for surgery include patients that require more than 4 units of blood in a 24-h period to remain hemodynamically stable, those in whom bleeding has not stopped after 72 h, or those who experience rebleeding within 1 week after an initial episode.
