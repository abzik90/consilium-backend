Angiodysplasias are acquired lesions with submucosal dilated and tortuous vessels that most commonly occur in the cecum and ascending colon, but can occur in the upper gastrointestinal tract as well. When this occurs they are most commonly noted in the stomach or duodenum. Bleeding from these lesions is intermittent and of varying intensity, but the majority of the bleeds from these lesions spontaneously cease. Uncontrolled bleeding of this source can be severe enough to be catastrophic, and given the intermittent nature of these lesions, they may be missed on endoscopy
