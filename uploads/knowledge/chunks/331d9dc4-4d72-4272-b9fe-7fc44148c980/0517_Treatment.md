Once bleeding hemorrhoids are identified, there are several options for treatment depending on the location of the hemorrhoids, the amount of bleeding present, and patient history/comorbidities (Fig. 3). The options range from lifestyle modifications to minimally invasive incision and drainage of thrombosed external hemorrhoids to rubber-band ligation to stapled or open hemorrhoidectomy for internal disease.
