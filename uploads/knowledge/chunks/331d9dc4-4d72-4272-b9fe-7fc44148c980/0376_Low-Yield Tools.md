Technetium-labeled nuclear scan. Similar to angiography, technetium-labeled red blood cell scans are most useful in patients who are actively bleeding, and it can detect bleeding rates as low as 0.1 mL/min [20]. While the sensitivity for detecting active bleeding exceeds that of angiography, localization of bleeding within the small bowel is poor. In addition, a positive scan can neither provide the etiology of GI bleeding nor be utilized for therapeutic intervention [19].
