heading: "Pathophysiology"
---

There are various etiologies to HP. Pseudoaneurysms of the common hepatic, gastroduodenal, pancreaticoduodenal, or splenic arteries have been reported [14-17]. Acute or chronic pancreatitis is the most common cause of pseudoaneurysm formation [18]. Pseudoaneurysms occur due to exocrine enzyme autodigestion and erosion into peripancreatic vessels [14, 18]. The most common cause of hemorrhage is due to a rupture of the splenic artery (60-65%) followed by gastroduodenal (20-25%), pancreaticoduodenal (10-15%), and hepatic artery (5-10%) [17, 1921]. Ruptured pseudoaneurysms portend a poor prognosis with a reported mortality of 12-57% [17]. Other uncommon causes include pancreatic pseudocysts and pancreaticolithiasis [22, 23].
