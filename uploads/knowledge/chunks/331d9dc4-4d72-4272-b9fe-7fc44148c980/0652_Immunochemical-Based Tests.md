heading: "Immunochemical-Based Tests"
---

The principal behind immunochemical tests is the use of antibodies directed against human globin epitopes to detect colonic blood [10]. Because globin molecules are degraded in the upper gastrointestinal tract, this test is useful only in the evaluation of lower gastrointestinal bleeding. The test is highly sensitive for the detection of colonic blood [11], however, it is quite cumbersome for the physician to perform. Room temperature storing of the sample must be avoided as loss of hemoglobin antigenicity may occur. The sample cannot be processed in the physician's office, instead needing to go to a special laboratory for processing.
