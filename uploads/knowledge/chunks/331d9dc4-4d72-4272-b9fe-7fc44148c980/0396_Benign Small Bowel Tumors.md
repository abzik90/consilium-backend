---

Hemangioma . These rare benign intestinal tumors represent less than 0.05% of all intestinal neoplasms. They are generally present in patients aged 5-25 years. They may become large and cause massive occult bleeding as well as abdominal pain or obstruction. Multiple small bowel hemangiomas have been described in association with blue rubber bleb syndrome, Maffucci syndrome, KlippelTrénaunay-Weber syndrome, and Peutz-Jeghers syndrome (Table 2) [49]. Bleeding hemangiomas should be treated either endoscopically with photocoagulation or argon laser coagulation or via surgical resection [27].
