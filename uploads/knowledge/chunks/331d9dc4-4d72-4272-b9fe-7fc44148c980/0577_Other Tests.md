Radionuclide scanning using technetium 99m Tc-labeled red blood cells or 99m Tc sulfur colloid is helpful in detecting active bleeding. The advantage of Technetium scintigraphy is that it can detect bleeding at a rate of 0.05-0.1 mL/min [15]. Furthermore, the tracer persists in the blood stream long after injection, which is particularly useful if the bleeding is intermittent in nature.
