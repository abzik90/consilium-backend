One alternative in these circumstances is direct ligation of the feeding hepatic artery [26]. Occasionally, hemobilia will be associated with a large intrahepatic defect. While radical surgical approaches have been employed in the treatment of hemobilia [27, 28], they are rarely indicated. The optimal treatment in cases with large defects is hepatic resection incorporating the defect and pseudo-aneurysm within the resection zone [3]. Since the portal triad gives rise to the successive branching pattern that forms the basis of hepatic anatomy, and since these injuries usually involve adjacent biliary and vascular structures, segmental resections should adequately control the hemorrhage while preserving hepatic parenchyma during urgent operative intervention.
