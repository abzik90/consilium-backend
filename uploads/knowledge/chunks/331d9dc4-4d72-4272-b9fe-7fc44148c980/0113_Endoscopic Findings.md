They found that most ulcers with a clean base, are associated with a 5% risk of rebleed and 2% mortality. Patients with ulcers that have a flat, pigmented spot on endoscopy have a 10% risk of further bleeding and 3% mortality. The presence of adherent clots on top of an ulcer is associated with a 22% risk of further bleeding and 7% mortality. A visible, nonbleeding vessel is correlated with a 43% risk of rebleed and 11% mortality, while actively bleeding vessels have the highest risk of recurrence at about 55% and a mortality of 11% [1]. Other lesions such as Mallory-Weiss tears are associated with a low risk (2%) of further bleeding [14]. These associations suggest that proper evaluation via endoscopy is crucial, as endoscopic findings are directly associated with patients' prognosis and therefore will aid in decisions concerning therapy.
