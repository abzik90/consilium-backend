heading: "Diverticular Disease"
---

Most commonly, lower GI bleeding from diverticular disease occurs in the form of bright red-colored stools but bleeding can also be sudden and massive compromising the hemodynamic state of the patient. These patients typically require intensive care admission with transfusion of blood and correction of any coagulopathy that might co-exist. In patients in whom the bleeding is not too significant, hemorrhage will cease in about 90% of the time. The recurrence rate over time after the first episode of bleeding is 9% at 1 year, 10% at 2 years, and 90% at 3 years [7].
