When hemobilia results form penetrating or blunt trauma, the diagnostic suspicion is usually confirmed by angiography. This is particularly useful in these cases since the diagnostic technique also serves as a therapeutic intervention. Angiography will commonly demonstrate a hepatic artery aneurysm or pseudoaneurysm that can be embolized with steel coils, gelfoam, or acrylate [2, 4].
