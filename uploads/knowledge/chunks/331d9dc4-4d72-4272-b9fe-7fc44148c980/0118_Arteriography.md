heading: "Arteriography"
---

Arteriography is an invasive, contrasted radiologic study that can identify briskly bleeding lesions, when the bleeding rate is 0.5 mL/min or greater. In the setting of upper GI bleeding, arteriography is positive for extravasation or abnormal mucosa blush in up to 61% of cases [17]. Some suggest that it has utility in locating structural abnormalities that may not be actively bleeding, such as angiodysplasias, tumors, or inflammatory lesions as well [18].
